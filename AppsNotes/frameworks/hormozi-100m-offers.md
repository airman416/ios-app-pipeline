# $100M Offers — Alex Hormozi (Key Extracts for iOS App System)

Source: Full book read. Apply to Stage 1 (Ideation) and Stage 3 (Monetization).

## Core Thesis
Stop competing on price. Compete on value. Charge as much as humanly possible while still making the purchase feel like a steal. The gap between cost-to-deliver and price charged is what makes a business scalable.

## The Grand Slam Offer
An offer so good people feel stupid saying no. Rare. Takes skill. No more effort to make than a mediocre offer — only skill differs.

Components: pricing + value + guarantees + naming.

## The Value Equation
```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement)
        / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

**Four drivers:**
1. Dream Outcome (increase) — what does the user's life look like after?
2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (increase) — will it work for me?
3. Time Delay (decrease) — how long until I see results?
4. Effort & Sacrifice (decrease) — how hard is it?

**The winning move:** Focus on the denominator. Making bigger promises is easy and cheap. Reducing time-to-value and effort is hard — which is why it's defensible.

**For apps:** The user should get something valuable in the first session. Every extra tap, screen, or permission request is friction that reduces value.

**Perception is reality.** A London tube map showing train arrival times increased satisfaction more than faster trains (billions in cost). Psychological solutions > logical solutions.

**The zero denominator:** If time delay = 0 and effort = 0, value = infinite. This is the theoretical limit every app should pursue.

## Market Selection
Three evergreen markets: **Health, Wealth, Relationships**. Find a submarket within one that is:
- In significant pain
- Has purchasing power
- Easy to target (reachable via ads/social)
- Growing

For apps: US consumer market, paid via App Store, reached via TikTok/Instagram.

## The 5-Step Grand Slam Offer Process

**Step 1: Dream Outcome**
What does the user achieve? Sell the vacation, not the plane. The outcome they want to *experience*, not the features you provide.

**Step 2: List Problems**
Every obstacle between user and outcome, mapped to 4 value drivers:
- This won't financially be worth it (Dream Outcome)
- It won't work for me specifically (Likelihood)
- This will be too hard / I'll quit (Effort)
- This will take too long / I'm too busy (Time)

Aim for 30–60 specific problems. More problems = more solutions = more value.

**Step 3: Problems → Solutions**
Reverse each problem into a solution: "How to X without Y, even if Z."
This becomes the feature checklist and the marketing copy.

**Step 4: Delivery Vehicles**
How could you solve each problem? Brainstorm widely:
- One-to-one (personal, expensive)
- Small group
- One-to-many (recorded, automated — best for apps)

Software is ideal: high one-time creation cost, zero marginal cost. Aim for solutions that scale infinitely.

**Step 5: Trim & Stack**
- Remove: high cost + low value
- Remove: low cost + low value
- Keep: low cost + high value ✓
- Keep: high cost + high value (only if massive value)

Stack the keepers. This is your feature set and offer bundle.

## Enhancing the Offer
- **Scarcity**: limited access, cohort-based starts
- **Urgency**: time-limited bonuses (today only)
- **Bonuses**: tools and checklists > additional training (lower effort = higher value)
- **Guarantees**: reduce risk perception ("if you don't X in Y days, Z")

Bonus value should *eclipse* core offer value psychologically.

## M-A-G-I-C Naming Formula
- **M**agnet — reason why ("Free", "Limited", "7-Day")
- **A**vatar — who it's for ("for women over 40", "for couples")
- **G**oal — dream outcome ("sleep through the night", "lose 20lbs")
- **I**nterval — timeline ("in 21 days", "in 6 weeks")
- **C**ontainer — delivery method ("program", "tracker", "system")

Names fatigue. Refresh with new wrapper (same offer, new name) when conversion drops.

## Key Quote
*"The pain is the pitch."* If you can articulate the pain accurately, they almost always buy.
