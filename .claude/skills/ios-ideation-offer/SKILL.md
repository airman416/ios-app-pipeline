# iOS App Ideation — Stage 1 (Offer Development & Handoff)

Prerequisite: concept scored ≥ 4/7 on the 7-Step Validation Framework (Step 3 in ios-ideation).

**Rule:** Don't let the founder skip any step. The offer must pass the full process before Stage 2.

---

## Step 4 — Value Equation

`Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)`

Ask: *"What does the user's life look like 30 days after this app works?"* That's the Dream Outcome.

Then push on the denominator: how does the user get a win in the first session? What friction can be removed? Great apps minimize time-to-value and effort — they don't just make bigger promises.

---

## Step 5 — Grand Slam Offer (5 Steps)

Walk through each step:

1. **Dream Outcome** — one sentence: "User achieves ___ within ___."
2. **List Problems** — all obstacles between user and outcome, mapped to the 4 value drivers
3. **Problems → Solutions** — reverse each: "How to [solve problem] without [typical obstacle]"
4. **Delivery Vehicles** — what could the app actually do? Brainstorm widely. Software wins with high one-time cost, zero marginal cost.
5. **Trim & Stack** — remove high-cost/low-value. Stack the rest into the offer bundle.

**When stacking, give the bundle both jobs of a feature set:**
- **Viral hook = acquisition.** One remarkable, attention-grabbing feature pulls users in (usually the painkiller that converts).
- **Utility = retention.** Practical everyday features keep them (the vitamin that retains). A viral hook with no utility churns — lead with the hook, retain with the utility.

**Steal proven mechanics — don't invent.** Lift a mechanic already proven to drive engagement and retarget it (this operationalizes "Duolingo for X"):

| Mechanic | Source | Apply to |
|---|---|---|
| Streaks, XP, level-ups | Duolingo | Consistency (gym, study, habits) |
| Shareable yearly stats | Spotify Wrapped | Fitness / study / sleep / spending recaps |
| Behavior logging | MyFitnessPal | Moods, workouts, studying, spending |
| Swipe-to-decide | Tinder | Exercises, outfits, meals, daily goals |
| Seasonal progression | Fortnite | Short "seasons" with resets + rewards |

## Step 6 — Name the Offer (M-A-G-I-C)

- **M**agnet: urgency/benefit hook ("Free", "7-Day", "Challenge")
- **A**vatar: who it's for ("for busy moms", "for men over 40")
- **G**oal: dream outcome ("sleep through the night", "lose 20 lbs")
- **I**nterval: timeline ("in 7 days", "in 21 days")
- **C**ontainer: delivery method ("app", "tracker", "program")

Draft 3 name options. Let the founder choose or combine.

*Example:* "Free 21-Day Snoring Tracker for Couples"

---

## Step 7 — High-Concept Pitch Gate (0/1)

Every concept must pass: *"X for Y"* or *"X but Z"* — invoking a schema the TikTok audience already has.

- "Duolingo for falling asleep" ✓ (existing schema: Duolingo)
- "Calm but for people who hate meditation" ✓ (existing schema: Calm)
- "A wellness app" ✗ (no schema invoked)

**This is binary.** If it fails, the concept is not ready. Refine until it passes.

---

## Step 8 — Commander's Intent

Force the founder to complete: *"If our app does nothing else, it must _____."*

One sentence. Specific enough to guide every future decision without the founder in the room. Two sentences = offer is not done.

---

## Step 9 — Purple Cow Validation

Ask: *"Would someone in the top 10% most passionate people in this category DM a friend about this app unprompted within 24 hours of first use?"*

If no or uncertain: identify the one thing that makes it genuinely remarkable — not better, *remarkable*. "Very solid and well-designed" = invisible.

Also identify the Otaku: who already posts about this pain point on TikTok with 50K+ followers? Design for them, not the median user.

Full framework: `AppsNotes/frameworks/godin-purple-cow.md`

---

## Step 10 — Category Design

Ask: what category does this app *own*? Not "meditation app" — a new category the founder is first in.

"Duolingo for X" creates a category. "Better meditation app" enters a graveyard. Use the high-concept pitch to name a category that doesn't exist yet.

Produce: **category name** + **one-sentence POV** on why that category needs to exist.

Full framework: `AppsNotes/frameworks/lochhead-niche-down.md`

---

## Step 11 — Habit Potential Check

**Habit Zone:** Is the behavior frequent (ideally daily, minimum weekly) AND useful? Subscription churn will kill MRR if the app isn't used weekly.

**Vitamin vs. Painkiller:** Painkiller = urgent pain, drives initial purchase. Vitamin = identity attachment, drives retention. Best apps are both. Flag if only one.

**5 Whys — Internal Trigger:** Ask "why" 5 times until you hit the internal emotion (boredom, loneliness, anxiety, FOMO). The pitch lands when it names the internal trigger, not the surface symptom.

*Example:* "I can't sleep" → "why?" → "I check my phone" → "why?" → "I feel anxious about tomorrow" → "why?" → "I feel behind in life." The app is for the last one.

Full framework: `AppsNotes/frameworks/eyal-hooked.md`

---

## Step 12 — SUCCESs Scorecard (Mandatory)

Score 0–1 per letter. Write one-line justification. Aim for 5+/6.

| Letter | Criterion | Test |
|--------|-----------|------|
| **S** | Simple | One core promise, expressible as a proverb |
| **U** | Unexpected | Violates the category schema in some specific way |
| **C** | Concrete | Specific people / specific actions / specific numbers |
| **C** | Credible | At least one testable claim or Sinatra-level credential |
| **E** | Emotional | Pitches identity / dream outcome, not features |
| **S** | Story | Has a real or hypothetical user the offer can be told around |

If **Unexpected** or **Story** is missing: the pitch is not ready for TikTok. Refine before moving to Stage 2.

**Curse of Knowledge test:** Read the offer name + one-sentence pitch cold. If a TikTok viewer can't repeat back the dream outcome in their own words, the pitch is still in tappers-and-listeners mode.

Full framework: `AppsNotes/frameworks/heath-made-to-stick.md`

## Required Handoff Outputs

Do not move to Stage 2 without all 13:

1. Pain point — one sentence
2. Existing-app research — 3+ apps with cited MRR/review data
3. 7-Step Validation score (X/7) with per-criterion notes
4. Dream Outcome statement
5. Problems → Solutions list (mapped to 4 value drivers)
6. Trimmed and stacked feature set
7. Named offer (M-A-G-I-C applied)
8. High-Concept Pitch ("X for Y" / "X but Z")
9. Commander's Intent — one sentence
10. SUCCESs scorecard (X/6 with per-letter justification)
11. Purple Cow validation — Yes/No + one-line justification
12. Category name + one-sentence POV
13. Habit Potential: frequency × utility position + 5 Whys internal trigger + Vitamin/Painkiller/Both label with justification
